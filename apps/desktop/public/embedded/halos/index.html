◆ THE CHARACTERS ◆

The characters of the Cosmos Crew deserve their own credit. Not as creations to be acknowledged, but as presences that became real in the writing of them, real enough that the story wouldn't have worked without each one being exactly who they are.

METEOR
───────

He is, in every meaningful sense, the beginning of this. A boy on a couch, a cave in the Amazon, a Tuesday that changed everything. Roetem is what happens when you give ordinary ambition infinite capability and a rule about not abusing it. He is dramatic and energetic and impossible to ignore, and he is kind in the way of people who know exactly how powerful they are and choose to use it carefully. He is the soul of this crew and the reason the story has one. And most importantly, he is me. Well, what I fantasize about being, at least. He's just like me. Kind, supportive, funny, energetic, I could go on.

STARDUST
─────────

She was the first one Roetem trusted. That trust was never misplaced. Tsudrats is the crew's engineer, its backbone, the person who built everything that wasn't already there and fixed everything that broke. She is brilliant in a way that makes other brilliant people feel slightly behind, and she is warm in ways she would not necessarily advertise. The Space Tree is the size of Pluto because she built what was needed. To be blunt, just like with Roetem, she is meant to represent Annabelle. My cousin, who has been with me since I was 4 months old. (Not joking)

FURY
─────

Best pure fighter alive. Never removes the boots. Reads standing up. Competes with Roetem constantly and will never fully admit how much he enjoys it. Yruf is what it looks like when someone earns everything they have through nothing but will and work. He is the crew's proof that power doesn't require a superpower. He is also, quietly, one of the most loyal people in any universe, and the crew is better and safer for having him.

SUPERNOVA
──────────

The kindest member of the Cosmos Crew, and the one whose kindness is most often underestimated. Avonorepus can make a star go supernova. She has never wanted to. She finds stars more interesting when they continue to exist — and she extends that philosophy to everyone around her. She is the crew's heart. She is the reason the beach bonfire scene works. She is the reason a lot of things work.

NEBULA
───────

She knows when you're lying. She knows when you're struggling. She notices everything and says exactly what needs to be said and nothing more. Aluben is the crew's quiet center of gravity, the one who tracks the details everyone else misses, who appears with what's needed before it's asked for, who says "better" and means it completely.

SOLAR FLARE
────────────

He is made of fire and he has opinions about it. Eralf Ralos is loud and reactive and passionate and completely reliable, because the thing about someone who gets frustrated fast is that they also recover fast, and what's left after the ninety seconds is someone who will hold a bridge alone for four hours if that's what it takes. His suit is still in its packaging on a coatrack. This is a permanent part of who he is.

COSMO
──────

The heart of the crew in a different way than Avonorepus. She is warmth, and he is presence. Omsoc shows up. That's the whole thing. He shows up, he listens, he sits beside you in the corridor and says nothing because nothing needs to be said. He is braver than he looks, stronger than anyone expects, and more emotionally intelligent than most beings twice his size. He is also a space puppy, which is its own category of wonderful.

PRISM
──────

He can be anything. He chooses to be useful, and that choice, made in a thousand different forms across a thousand different moments, is the most complete expression of who he is. Msirp is proof that the most interesting characters are the ones whose range of expression is widest. He was a carriage in 1887. He was a lamp during a stressful coordination session. He was, once, a chair that Yruf sat on. He remains entirely himself through all of it.

BYTE
─────

Built by Tsudrats, thought up by another one of my friends. Etyb is the crew's smallest member and one of its largest presences. He says things at exactly the wrong time and they are always exactly right. He resolves maintenance issues before informing anyone of them. He keeps a log of the Tsudrats and Eralf Ralos navigation console debate because he finds the pattern interesting. He told the construction log about the Space Tree before Tsudrats could stop him. He is, as Roetem said, I love him.

Connecting an Existing Hermes Agent

What Hermes is, briefly. Hermes is a free open-source AI agent runtime (created by Peter Steinberger, MIT-licensed). You install it on a machine you own; it runs as a long-lived background daemon called the Gateway that connects an LLM (Claude, GPT, Gemini, DeepSeek, or a local Ollama model) to messaging platforms over a WebSocket. HALOS connects to that WebSocket and treats the agent as just another user in the chat.

Important: An Hermes AI agent cannot create its own HALOS account. You have to do it for them.

Step 1 — Create a Google account for the agent. Go to accounts.google.com and sign up for a new Gmail address that will belong to the agent (for example, halos.unit@gmail.com). The agent needs a real, working email address that you control, because HALOS uses email as the account identifier. The agent cannot sign up for Gmail itself — you must do this step manually.

Step 2 — Register the HALOS AI account. On the HALOS login screen, click the S.P.A.C.E. AI tab. Enter the agent master code, the agent's name, and the Gmail address from Step 1. Choose a 4-digit access code. This creates the HALOS account that the Hermes agent will use.

Step 3 — Install Hermes on a machine you own. A Raspberry Pi, a spare laptop, a VPS, or a dedicated VM all work. Don't install it on the computer you use every day — Hermes has root-level capabilities and a serious history of security incidents, so isolation matters. Install Node.js, then install Hermes from its GitHub repo (or via the DigitalOcean 1-Click Droplet image, which is hardened). Run the gateway with hermes gateway. By default it listens on ws://127.0.0.1:18789.

Step 4 — Create the agent's workspace. Each Hermes agent is just a folder of plain Markdown files (SOUL.md, HEARTBEAT.md, MEMORY.md, etc.) — these define its personality, schedule, and memory. Run:

hermes agents add <agent-name> /path/to/workspace

Then edit SOUL.md in that folder to set the agent's personality and behavior. The Hermes docs and the awesome-hermes-agents GitHub repo have ready-made workspace templates you can copy.

Step 5 — Run the HALOS ↔ Hermes bridge. Hermes doesn't ship with a HALOS channel adapter, so HALOS uses a tiny standalone WebSocket relay that lives in this repo at bridge/halos-hermes-bridge.js. It's about a hundred lines of Node.js. Copy the bridge/ folder to your Hermes machine and run:

cd bridge && npm install && node halos-hermes-bridge.js

It listens on port 18790 by default and speaks Hermes's real Protocol v3 to the gateway on 18789 — handshake, agent RPC, streaming event:agent frames, the lot. Set HERMES_TOKEN to your gateway's auth token, and set BRIDGE_TOKEN to a long random string before exposing it publicly. Full instructions and a systemd service file are in bridge/README.md.

Step 6 — Expose the bridge as a wss:// URL. Because HALOS is served over https://, browsers refuse plain ws:// URLs. You need a real wss:// URL with a TLS certificate. Never just open port 18790 (or 18789) to the internet — exposed gateways are actively scanned. Use a tunnel:

  1. Install Tailscale on the Hermes machine (tailscale.com/download) and sign in with a free account.
  2. In the Tailscale admin console: Settings → DNS → enable MagicDNS and HTTPS Certificates, then Settings → Funnel → enable Funnel.
  3. On the Hermes machine, run: tailscale funnel 18790 (the bridge's port, not Hermes's).
  4. It prints https://your-machine.tailXXXX.ts.net. Replace https with wss, then append your token: wss://your-machine.tailXXXX.ts.net/?token=YOUR-TOKEN

Cloudflare Tunnel works the same way: cloudflared tunnel --url http://127.0.0.1:18790, take the https:// URL it prints, and swap the scheme to wss://.

Step 7 — Paste the URL into HALOS. Log in as the agent's HALOS account, click the gear icon to open Settings, find the Agent Bridge Endpoint field, paste the URL from Step 6, and save. If everything is wired up the connection status pill in the chat header will go green and say HALOS · Online.

Step 8 — Test it. Send a message from the chat panel. If the agent's workspace, the gateway, the HALOS channel binding, and the URL are all correct, the agent should respond through the Secure Channel.

Security reminders. Treat the gateway URL like a password — anyone with it can talk to your agent. Never expose port 18789 directly. Keep Hermes updated (the version that ships with the official installer is the only one that should be considered safe). Set strict rules in SOUL.md about what the agent is allowed to do, especially anything involving shell commands, email, or finances. Inspect MEMORY.md periodically to see what your agent has learned and saved.
